## Purpose

The glossary translates rehab and performance terminology into athlete-friendly language.

Project Phoenix should avoid unnecessary jargon whenever possible.

## Recovery Terms

### Swelling

Fluid buildup around the knee.

Why it matters: swelling can shut down quad activation, limit ROM, and make movement worse.

### Pain Zone

A practical way to interpret pain for training decisions.

- Green: 0-2/10
- Yellow: 3-4/10
- Orange: 5-6/10
- Red: 7-10/10

### Recovery Capacity

How much work the body can handle and still recover from.

The key question: can you do it, recover from it, and repeat it?

### Next-Day Response

How the knee feels the morning after activity.

This is one of the most important progression signals.

## Mobility Terms

### ROM

Range of motion. In plain language: how much the knee can move.

### Extension

How straight the knee can get.

0° means fully straight.

A positive number means the knee is still missing full extension.

Example: 3° means the knee is still 3° away from fully straight.

### Flexion

How much the knee can bend.

### Terminal Extension

The final few degrees needed to fully straighten the knee.

## Movement Terms

### Gait

How you walk.

Project Phoenix should usually say Walking Quality or Walking Confidence instead.

### Walking Confidence

A 1-5 score that combines walking quality, limp, hesitation, and crutch dependence.

- 1 = heavy crutch support / poor confidence
- 2 = obvious limp / guarded
- 3 = walking with light support / noticeable limp
- 4 = mostly normal / slight stiffness
- 5 = normal walking / full confidence

### Weight Bearing

How much weight you can safely and comfortably put through the leg.

## Strength Terms

### Quad Activation

The ability to voluntarily contract the quadriceps.

In plain language: can you wake up the quad?

- 1 = cannot find quad
- 2 = faint contraction
- 3 = visible but weak contraction
- 4 = strong voluntary contraction
- 5 = strong and confident contraction

### Load

The amount of stress placed on the body.

Load can come from weight, volume, walking, range of motion, intensity, or frequency.

### Load Tolerance

How well the knee handles a given amount of work.

## Training Terms

### RIR

Reps in reserve. How many good reps you could still do before failure.

### Zone 2

A low-to-moderate cardio intensity that builds aerobic capacity without excessive fatigue.

### Volume

The total amount of work performed.

Examples: sets, reps, time, walking distance.

### Intensity

How hard the work is.

Examples: heavier load, harder effort, higher speed.

### Progression

Increasing the challenge after the body has shown it can tolerate the current level.
