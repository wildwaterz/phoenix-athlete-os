# Evidence Notes

Version: 1.0
Owner: Kevin Sauvageau
Product: Project Phoenix OS
Status: Living Document

## Purpose

This document stores evidence notes that support Project Phoenix coaching rules.

This is not a formal systematic review. It is a living evidence map used to keep product and coaching decisions honest.

## Evidence Philosophy

When evidence is precise, be precise.

When evidence supports a range, use a range.

When evidence is uncertain, say so.

## Protein

Current coaching target:

- Practical target: around 170-190 g/day
- Minimum acceptable range for Kevin: often 150-170 g/day
- Rationale: supports muscle retention, recovery, and rehab during reduced lower-body loading

Evidence summary:

- Strength athletes often benefit from protein intakes above the RDA.
- Muscle hypertrophy benefits tend to plateau around 1.6 g/kg/day, with some individuals benefiting up to about 2.2 g/kg/day.
- During calorie restriction or injury recovery, higher intakes may be useful.

Product implication:

Use protein zones rather than a single pass/fail target.

## Swelling

Coaching rule:

Do not progress aggressively when swelling increases.

Rationale:

Swelling can inhibit quad activation, reduce ROM, and worsen movement quality.

Product implication:

Swelling should be a core readiness metric.

## Pain

Coaching rule:

Pain is interpreted by zone and response, not number alone.

Product implication:

Track pain during activity, after activity, and next morning.

## Extension

Coaching rule:

Early restoration of knee extension is a high priority.

Rationale:

Extension supports walking quality, quad function, and later strengthening.

Product implication:

Extension should remain visible on the Dashboard during early phases.

## Next-Day Response

Coaching rule:

The next morning matters more than the workout itself.

Rationale:

A session is only well tolerated if the knee recovers from it.

Product implication:

Morning Coach Packet must include previous evening response and trends.
