Version: 1.0
Owner: Kevin Sauvageau
Product: Project Phoenix OS
Status: Living Document

## Purpose

Milestones define evidence-based progression points.

They turn rehab from a calendar-based process into a competency-based process.

## Milestone Structure

Each milestone should include:

- Name
- Description
- Why it matters
- Evidence required
- Status
- Date unlocked
- Coach notes
- Related mission

## Status Values

- Locked
- In progress
- Unlocked

## Initial ACL Campaign Milestones

### Stable Pain Baseline

Description:

Pain is controlled and not escalating across daily check-ins.

Evidence required:

- Pain in green or low yellow zone
- No meaningful pain increase next morning
- No concerning symptoms

### Swelling Under Control

Description:

Swelling is stable or improving.

Evidence required:

- Swelling score stable or decreasing
- No increase after daily activity
- Walking confidence not worsening

### Quad Activation 4/5

Description:

The athlete can voluntarily activate the quad with a strong contraction.

Evidence required:

- Quad activation scored 4/5 or better
- Repeatable across multiple sessions
- No compensation or pain escalation

### Straight Leg Raise Without Lag

Description:

The athlete can lift the leg while keeping the knee fully controlled.

Evidence required:

- No visible extension lag
- Quad remains engaged
- No pain escalation
- Physio approval if required

### Passive Extension to 0°

Description:

The knee can reach full passive extension.

Evidence required:

- Extension recorded as 0°
- No increase in swelling after extension work
- Stable across repeated check-ins

### 30-Minute Pain-Free Walk

Description:

The athlete can walk 30 minutes without pain or next-day swelling response.

Evidence required:

- Pain stays green
- Walking confidence remains stable
- No swelling increase next morning

## Milestone Principle

A milestone is not earned because the athlete performed it once.

A milestone is earned when the athlete can perform it, recover from it, and repeat it.
