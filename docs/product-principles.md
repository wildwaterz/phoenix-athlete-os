# Product Principles

Version: 1.1  
Owner: Kevin Sauvageau  
Product: Project Phoenix OS  
Status: Living Document

## 1. Evidence Beats Ego

Progression is earned through evidence, not optimism, impatience, or the calendar.

## 2. The Calendar Does Not Promote You

Time passing is not enough to unlock the next step. The athlete progresses when symptoms, movement quality, tolerance, and milestones support it.

## 3. Phase-Aware, Not Static

The app must evolve as rehab evolves.

Early rehab may care about walking confidence. Later rehab should care about movement quality, session tolerance, strength capacity, and sport confidence.

## 4. Tracks Can Progress in Parallel

Recovery is not one straight line.

Symptoms, ROM, activation, walking, capacity, and sport confidence can improve at different speeds.

The app should support parallel tracks rather than forcing every mission into one linear ladder.

## 5. ROM Means Extension and Flexion

Extension is critical, but flexion matters too.

The product should track and support both straightening and bending the knee.

## 6. Readiness Is Contextual

A symptom score alone is not enough.

Example: swelling 5/10 on Day 1 after surgery may be expected surgical swelling. Swelling 5/10 after a training session that started at 2/10 is a different signal.

## 7. AI Interprets; Project Phoenix Owns the Data

The app should not depend on AI to function.

Project Phoenix should generate structured coach packets, rule-based small wins, and transparent readiness outputs. AI can interpret exported data externally.

## 8. Quality Over Volume

A small amount of high-quality work that the knee tolerates is better than more work that causes swelling, compensation, or loss of confidence.
