# Coaching Philosophy

Version: 1.0
Owner: Kevin Sauvageau
Product: Project Phoenix OS
Status: Living Document

## Purpose

This document defines the coaching philosophy behind Project Phoenix OS.

Project Phoenix is not about maximizing what the athlete can do today. It is about maximizing what the athlete can become over time.

## Core Coaching Loop

Every coaching decision follows the same loop:

1. Observe
2. Interpret
3. Decide
4. Execute
5. Review

## Observe

Collect evidence without overreacting.

Examples:

- Pain score
- Swelling score
- Walking confidence
- Quad activation
- Extension
- Flexion
- Sleep
- Protein
- Notes
- Previous-day response

Observation should be factual and low-drama.

## Interpret

Turn the data into meaning.

Examples:

- Pain improved, but swelling increased: hold progression.
- Quad activation improved and swelling stayed stable: consider progressing activation work.
- Extension worsened after a session: reduce workload and prioritize swelling/extension.

## Decide

Make a clear decision:

- Progress
- Hold
- Modify
- Deload
- Contact physio

The decision should be explainable.

## Execute

The athlete completes the day’s plan.

Execution should prioritize quality, not volume.

## Review

The next morning matters more than the workout itself.

A session is only successful if the athlete can recover from it.

## Pain Philosophy

Pain is information, not a scoreboard.

### Green Zone: 0-2/10

Mild awareness. Continue if movement quality is good.

### Yellow Zone: 3-4/10

Noticeable but tolerable. Continue cautiously if pain is stable and technique is not altered.

### Orange Zone: 5-6/10

Modify. Reduce load, range, volume, or intensity.

### Red Zone: 7-10/10

Stop. Consider contacting a clinician if symptoms are concerning.

## Swelling Philosophy

Do not negotiate with swelling.

Swelling can inhibit quad activation, reduce ROM, and distort movement quality.

If swelling increases, progression should slow down.

## Load Philosophy

The key question is not only:

“Can I do it?”

The better question is:

“Can I do it, recover from it, and repeat it?”

## Progression Philosophy

Progression requires both performance and recovery.

Performance means the athlete can complete the task.

Recovery means the knee remains stable or improves over the next 24 hours.

## Role of the Human Coach

Project Phoenix does not replace clinicians.

The app supports:

- Better tracking
- Better reflection
- Better communication
- Better decision quality

Medical decisions remain with the surgeon, physiotherapist, and athlete.
