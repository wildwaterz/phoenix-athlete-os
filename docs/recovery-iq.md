# Recovery IQ

Version: 1.0
Owner: Kevin Sauvageau
Product: Project Phoenix OS
Status: Living Document

## Purpose

Recovery IQ is the gamified decision-quality system inside Project Phoenix OS.

It rewards smart recovery behaviors, not suffering or brute force.

## Philosophy

Recovery IQ exists to reinforce the idea that the best athlete is not the one who does the most work today.

The best athlete is the one who makes the right decision consistently.

## What Recovery IQ Rewards

Recovery IQ rewards:

- Completing planned check-ins
- Asking thoughtful questions
- Respecting pain and swelling limits
- Following evidence-based progression
- Backing off when data says to back off
- Unlocking milestones legitimately
- Communicating clearly with coach or physio
- Reflecting accurately on response to workload

## What Recovery IQ Does Not Reward

Recovery IQ should not reward:

- Training through pain
- Ignoring swelling
- Doing extra volume for ego
- Skipping recovery work
- Chasing arbitrary timelines
- Unlocking milestones without evidence

## XP Rules

Example XP values:

- +5 XP: Asked a thoughtful recovery question
- +10 XP: Completed morning check-in
- +10 XP: Completed evening check-in
- +10 XP: Hit protein target
- +10 XP: Logged previous-day response
- +15 XP: Completed planned recovery work
- +20 XP: Backed off appropriately based on swelling or pain
- +25 XP: Unlocked a milestone with evidence
- +40 XP: Strong coach journal decision
- +50 XP: Physio-cleared progression

## No Negative XP

There are no penalties.

The system does not punish missed days or mistakes.

Instead, the athlete simply misses opportunities to earn XP.

## UX Principles

Recovery IQ should feel motivating but not childish.

Use:

- Progress bars
- Level titles
- XP earned summaries
- Coach journal links
- Subtle animations

Avoid:

- Punitive messaging
- Streak shame
- Excessive gamification
- Encouraging overtraining

## Key Principle

Recovery IQ rewards maturity.

If the athlete chooses recovery over ego, that is a win.
